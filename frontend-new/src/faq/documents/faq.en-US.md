## 1. About Njila

### What is Njila?

Njila is a free AI career guidance platform built for the Zambian youth, supported by a collaboration between the Ministry of Technical and Vocational Skills Development (MoTS) of Zambia and the World Bank Group. Through a suite of interactive chatbots, Njila helps users identify and log the skills they have, explore career paths that match those skills, and identify opportunities that match their skills and interests.

### Who can use Njila?

Njila has been built for Zambian students, graduates, and young people who are looking for work, including anyone who wants to understand their skills and career options better. It is free to use and does not require prior work experience.

Users must be at least 16 years old to use Njila. Users under the age of 18 may use the Service only with the consent and active supervision of a parent or legal guardian.

### What can I do with Njila?

Njila is a platform that can help you get prepared for the job market.

- Understand and articulate your experiences from school, training, work, home life, or community into the skills that employers value.
- Access interactive chat agents dedicated to preparing users for work through practical learning modules such as resume and cover letter writing, workplace readiness, and interview preparation.
- Explore what career pathways exist across sectors in Zambia, particularly in national priority and growth sectors such as agriculture, mining, hospitality, energy, and more.
- Browse and get matched to real job openings that fit the user's skills, interests, and training, delivered through the platform.

### Do I have to pay to use Njila?

No. Njila is completely free to use. There are no fees for creating an account, chatting with the AI coach, or downloading a skills report.

## 2. Getting Started: Registration & Log In

### It says my account is not verified when I try to log in.

When an account is registered with email/password, Njila requires email verification. Open the email from "Njila", then click the verification link to activate the account.

### I did not receive any verification email.

Sometimes verification emails can end up in different folders:

- Check the spam/junk folder of the email you used.
- Verify that you are checking the email address entered during registration.
- Wait a few minutes and check again — emails may be delayed.

### I tried to register, but it says my email is already in use.

This means an account with that email already exists. If you don't remember the password:

- Go to [https://njila.ai/](https://njila.ai/)
- Enter the email you were trying to create an account with.
- Click on "Forgot Password" under the Password field.
- You can also check if you logged into Njila previously using Google. On the log in page, click "Login with Google" and select the email.

### I forgot my password.

To reset your password:

1. Go to [https://njila.ai/](https://njila.ai/)
2. Click the "Forgot Password" link.
3. Enter the registered email address.
4. Click "Send Reset Email".
5. Check the email inbox for a password reset link.
6. Click the link and follow the instructions to create a new password.

### I did not receive a password reset email.

If the reset email was not received, you can try the following:

- Check the spam, junk, or promotions folders.
- Verify the email address entered is correct.
- Wait a few minutes and refresh your inbox — email delivery can be delayed.
- Resend the reset password after 60 seconds.

### The email verification link does not work when I click it.

- **Copy and paste:** instead of clicking, copy the full URL and paste it into the browser's address bar.
- **Check for line breaks:** email clients sometimes split long URLs across multiple lines — make sure the full URL is copied.
- **Request a new link:** the link may have expired; register again or request a new verification email.

### My account has been blocked after entering the wrong password too many times.

For security, Njila temporarily blocks login attempts from a device after repeated failed password entries. To resolve this:

- Wait a short period before trying again.
- Use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page to reset the password — this is the fastest way back in.
- If the block continues, or the account appears to be disabled, email [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai) for help.

## 3. Chatbot Not Responding

### The chatbot shows a loading indicator but never responds.

This usually indicates a network or server issue. Try:

- **Check internet connection:** ensure the connection is stable.
- **Refresh the page:** press F5 or Ctrl+R (Cmd+R on Mac).
- **Wait 30–120 seconds:** the AI may be processing a complex request.
- **Check the offline banner:** an offline notification at the top indicates the internet is disconnected.

### When I type a message and click send, nothing happens.

Check these common causes:

- **Offline:** look for an offline banner at the top of the page.
- **Message length:** messages must not exceed 1000 characters.
- **Button disabled:** the send button is disabled when offline, when there is no text, or when the text exceeds 1000 characters.

### My message was sent but the chatbot did not respond.

If the message appears but there is no response:

- Wait 30–120 seconds — AI processing can take time.
- Check for error messages in the chat.
- Refresh the page to see if the response appears.
- Check the network connection status.

### I see an error "Something went wrong" or "Cannot connect to the service".

These generic errors typically indicate:

- **Network issue:** "Cannot connect to the service" indicates an internet connection problem.
- **Server error:** the backend service may be experiencing issues.
- **Rate limiting:** too many requests in a short time.
- **Invalid input:** the message may contain unsupported characters or formatting.

To resolve:

- Check the internet connection.
- Wait 1–2 minutes and try again.
- Refresh the page.
- If the error persists, the service may be temporarily down.

### The chatbot takes a very long time to respond (30+ seconds).

Slow response times can be caused by factors on the user end:

- Poor mobile signal — switch to Wi-Fi if using cellular data.
- Slow internet connection — check connection speed.
- Network congestion on school or corporate networks during peak hours.
- Too many browser tabs — close unnecessary tabs to free up resources.

If responses consistently take more than 120 seconds, there may be a server issue.

### I can see the typing indicator but my previous messages aren't showing.

If conversation history fails to load:

- Refresh the page (F5).
- Check the internet connection.
- Clear the browser cache and reload.
- Log out and log back in.

For registered users, conversation history is stored permanently and should reload automatically.

### Can I use Njila on my phone or tablet?

Yes. Njila is fully responsive and works on:

- Smartphones (iOS, Android).
- Tablets (iPad, Android tablets).
- Desktop computers.

The interface adapts to screen size.

## 4. Conversation & Session Issues

### Can I access my conversations from a different device?

Yes, for registered accounts (email/password or Google Sign-In):

- Log in on the new device using the same credentials.
- Conversation history will load automatically.
- Sessions are synced across all devices.

### The chatbot is sending the same message multiple times.

Duplicate responses can occur when:

- **Send was clicked multiple times:** each click sends a new request.
- **Browser refresh during processing:** refreshing while waiting for a response.
- **Server-side error:** the backend may have processed the same message twice.

To prevent:

- Click the send button only once.
- Wait for the response before sending another message.
- Do not refresh the page while waiting for a response.

If duplicates persist, note the exact circumstances and report the issue.

### The chatbot contradicts itself or forgets information from earlier in the conversation.

This can happen in very long conversations due to:

- **Conversation summarization:** older details get compressed.
- **Context window limits:** the AI can only consider a certain amount of history.
- **Phase transitions:** moving to a new phase may shift focus.

If critical information is forgotten, remind the chatbot in a current message. The most recent exchanges have the strongest influence on responses.

## 5. Network & Connectivity

### Njila is very slow on my internet connection. Can I still use it?

Njila is designed to work on slow connections, though performance will be affected.

Tips for using Njila on a slow connection:

- Close other tabs or apps using bandwidth.
- Disable video streaming in other windows.
- Use Wi-Fi instead of cellular if available.
- Avoid peak hours on shared networks.
- Be patient — wait for full page load before interacting.

### Can I use Njila offline?

No, Njila requires an active internet connection. All conversation processing happens on the server, not locally.

### What happens if I lose my internet connection while using Njila?

When offline:

- A notification banner appears at the top of the screen indicating the offline state.
- All buttons and inputs are disabled — messages cannot be sent or navigation performed.
- The current conversation remains visible — message history can still be read.
- No data is lost — everything resumes on reconnection.

### I see an "offline" banner, but I'm connected to Wi-Fi.

This usually means the Wi-Fi is connected but the internet itself is not reaching the device. Things to try:

- Open another website (for example google.com) in a new tab — if it also fails, the problem is with the internet connection, not with Njila.
- Check whether other devices on the same Wi-Fi can reach the internet.
- Restart the router or Wi-Fi modem.
- Switch to mobile data to check whether the problem is with the Wi-Fi network.
- Some public Wi-Fi networks (at cafés, hotels, campuses) require signing in through a welcome page before the internet will work.

## 6. Data & Export Problems

### I want to download my skills report but can't find the option.

To download the skills report:

- Look for an "Experiences" or "Skills" section or drawer in the navigation.
- Find a "Download Report" or export button.
- Select a format — PDF or DOCX (Word document).
- The report should download automatically to the default downloads folder.

If no download option is visible:

- It may be necessary to progress further in the conversation (complete the skills discovery phase).
- The feature may not be enabled for the account type in use.
- Check that the device is not offline — download buttons are disabled when offline.

### I click download but get an error instead of a file.

Report generation errors can be caused by:

- **Missing data:** the conversation may not be complete enough to generate a report.
- **Browser compatibility:** very old browsers may not support PDF generation.
- **Memory issues:** very large reports may fail on low-memory devices.

Solutions:

- Ensure the skills discovery phase is complete.
- Update the browser to the latest version.
- Close other tabs to free up memory.
- Try DOCX format if PDF fails.
- Try a different device or browser.

### The PDF is blank or missing information.

If the report downloads but content is missing:

- Check that all required information was provided in the conversation.
- Scroll through the PDF — content may be on later pages.
- Try DOCX format to see if content appears there.
- Re-download — generation glitches are possible.
- Continue the conversation and try downloading again after adding more information.

### My skills report shows different information than what we discussed.

The report contains information confirmed during the conversation. Discrepancies may occur if:

- **Skills were added then removed:** only final confirmed skills appear.
- **Multiple edits:** the latest version of the data is used.
- **Different conversation sessions:** confirm the correct session is selected.
- **Data not confirmed:** information may have been discussed but not finalized.

If specific information is clearly wrong:

- Review the conversation to see what was actually confirmed.
- Continue the conversation and update or correct the information.
- Download the report again after making changes.

### Some of my experiences or skills are missing from the report.

Check whether:

- The relevant conversation phase was completed — only finalized experiences are included.
- Items were deleted during the conversation.
- The wrong session is active.
- A phase was not fully completed — some phases must be finished for data to export.

Review the conversation history to verify what was actually confirmed and finalized.

Njila is currently only available in English, but support for more languages may be added in the future. If a particular language would be useful for you, send feedback to [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai).

### How do I delete my account?

Account deletion requires a request to [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai). Once the request is received, the account will be deleted. See also "How do I access, correct, or delete my data?" in the Privacy & Terms section for related data rights.

## 8. Contacting Support

### I found a problem. How do I report it?

The fastest way to report a problem is the **Report a bug** button at the bottom-right of any page on Njila. The form lets you enter your name and email, describe the issue, and attach a screenshot — your report goes directly to the Njila team.

Alternatively, email [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai). The more detail shared, the faster the team can fix the issue. A good report includes:

- What you were trying to do.
- What happened instead.
- The exact text of any error message shown.
- What device and browser were in use (for example: "Android phone, Chrome" or "Windows laptop, Edge").
- A screenshot, if possible.
- When it happened (date and approximate time).

### How do I get in touch with the Njila team for other reasons?

For general questions, feedback, account issues, or data requests, email [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai).

## 9. Privacy & Terms

### What data does Njila collect about me?

Njila collects only the information needed to operate the service:

- **Account information:** name and email address.
- **Basic demographics:** age range, language preference, and general location.
- **Usage analytics:** how you interact with the platform, plus technical information such as device type, browser, and operating system.
- **Conversation content:** your messages and Njila's responses.
- **Skill outcomes:** results from skills assessments and career recommendations.

Njila does not collect sensitive personal data such as health information, biometric identifiers, or details about race or ethnicity. For the full list of data collected and how it is used, see the [Privacy Policy](https://njila.ai/#/privacy-policy).

### Is my conversation data private and secure?

Njila is operated by Tabiya, a project fiscally sponsored by the Global Development Incubator (GDI). Conversation data and personal information are encrypted in transit and at rest, and stored on Google Cloud servers. Sensitive demographic details (such as name and age) are encrypted on your device before being sent. Only you can see your own conversation history; authorized system administrators may access data for support and maintenance purposes.

Njila uses analytics tools to understand how the platform is used and to improve the experience. For full details on what data is collected, how it is used, and your rights, see the [Privacy Policy](https://njila.ai/#/privacy-policy).

### Who can see my conversation history?

Access to your conversations:

- **You:** full access to all conversations.
- **System administrators:** may have access for support or maintenance purposes.
- **Your institution (if applicable):** may have aggregate or anonymized data access depending on the agreement.
- **Other users:** cannot see your conversations.

Review the privacy policy for specific data access and retention policies.

### How do I access, correct, or delete my data?

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data Njila holds about you, to correct any inaccuracies, or to request deletion. To make any of these requests, email [hi@njila.ai](mailto:hi@njila.ai).

Njila will respond within the timeframe required by law and may need to verify your identity before disclosing or deleting personal data. Some information may need to be retained to meet legal obligations.

- [Terms of Use](https://njila.ai/#/terms-of-use)
- [Privacy Policy](https://njila.ai/#/privacy-policy)

Both documents are also linked from the footer of the Njila website. Review them to understand user rights and service obligations.

## 10. Quick Troubleshooting Checklist

Before contacting support, try these common fixes.

- Refresh the page (F5 or Ctrl+R / Cmd+R).
- Clear the browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Delete / Cmd+Shift+Delete).
- Log out and log back in.
- Close the browser completely and reopen.
- Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
- Check the internet connection (open another website).
- Disable browser extensions temporarily (especially ad blockers and privacy tools).
- Update the browser to the latest version.
- Restart the device.
