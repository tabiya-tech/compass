## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is collected, used, stored, and protected when you use Njila ("Njila" or the "Service"), an AI-powered career guidance and skills discovery platform designed for users in Zambia. This Policy applies to all users of Njila and forms part of our Terms of Use.

We are committed to complying with the Zambia Data Protection Act No. 3 of 2021 (the "DPA"), which is the primary law governing our processing of your personal data. As an additional commitment to data protection best practice, we have voluntarily designed our systems and policies to be substantively equivalent to the standards of the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (the "GDPR"). We are not legally bound by the GDPR, but where the GDPR provides a higher standard than the DPA, we will follow the GDPR standard.

## 2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect only the personal data we need to provide the Service. Specifically:

**Account information.** When you register for Njila, you provide us with your name, the name of your school or institution, your school year, and the course in which you are enrolled. This information is used to create your account and tailor the Service to your context.

**Skills, work, and learning history.** As you use Njila, you may share information about your skills, work experience (including informal, unpaid, and household work), education, training, and qualifications. This information is used to build a skills profile that helps you explore career pathways.

**Career preferences.** You may share information about the occupations, sectors, and types of work that interest you.

**Conversation content.** Njila uses conversational AI to help you discover and articulate your skills. The content of your conversations with Njila is processed to generate responses and may be retained to provide continuity across sessions and to improve the quality of the Service.

**Usage information.** We collect technical information about how you use Njila, including pages or screens you access, features you use, dates and times of access, and broad device and browser information. This is used for security, troubleshooting, and improvement of the Service.

**Communications with us.** If you contact us by email or through Njila, we retain those communications so we can respond and follow up.

### 2.1 Sensitive Personal Data

The DPA defines "sensitive personal data" to include data revealing race, ethnic origin, marital status, sex, political opinions, religious beliefs, health, biometric data, or trade union membership. Njila does **not** require you to provide sensitive personal data, and we do not intentionally collect it.

If, in the course of using Njila's free-text or conversational features, you voluntarily disclose information that falls within the DPA's definition of sensitive personal data, that information will be processed solely for the purpose of providing the Service to you and will not be used for any other purpose. We will not share such information with third parties except as described in this Policy.

We encourage you not to share sensitive personal information with Njila where it is not relevant to your skills or career exploration.

## 3. How We Use Your Personal Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

- To create and manage your account and authenticate you when you log in.
- To provide the core Service: helping you discover, articulate, and document your skills; guiding you through career exploration; and producing outputs such as skills profiles.
- To operate Njila's conversational AI features, including generating responses and recommendations.
- To improve Njila, including by analyzing how users interact with the Service and identifying areas for improvement. Where possible, this is done with aggregated or anonymized data.
- To communicate with you about the Service, including responding to your questions and notifying you of important changes.
- To monitor and protect the security and integrity of the Service, including detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, and misuse.
- To comply with applicable law, including the DPA, and to respond to lawful requests from authorities.
- To support research that advances Tabiya's charitable mission of expanding economic opportunity, where this is done using anonymized or aggregated data and consistent with applicable law.

## 4. Legal Bases for Processing

We process personal data on the following legal bases recognized under the DPA, applied in a manner consistent with the GDPR:

**Performance of a contract.** Most of the processing necessary to provide Njila to you is based on the agreement you enter into with us by accepting our Terms of Use. Without processing this data, we cannot provide the Service.

**Consent.** Where we ask for your consent — including for the cross-border transfer of your personal data described in Section 5, and for any optional features that go beyond the core Service — we will only process your data for those purposes if you give consent. You may withdraw consent at any time (see Section 9).

**Legitimate interests.** We process certain data on the basis of our legitimate interests, including to maintain the security of the Service, to prevent fraud and misuse, to improve the Service, and to conduct research that supports our charitable mission. Where we rely on legitimate interests, we balance our interests against your rights, and we will not process your data where your rights override our interests.

**Legal obligation.** We may process personal data where required by law, including to comply with the DPA, to respond to lawful requests from public authorities, or to defend against legal claims.

We do not use your personal data for automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you without human involvement. Njila's recommendations are advisory: you decide what to do with the information we provide.

## 5. Where Your Data Is Stored: Cross-Border Transfer

### 5.1 Storage location

Your personal data is stored on servers located in the **European Union (Netherlands)**. Specifically:

- **Application backend and data at rest:** Google Cloud Platform, region **`europe-west4`** (Netherlands).
- **Database:** MongoDB Atlas, hosted on Google Cloud Platform in the European Union.
- **AI processing:** Google Cloud Vertex AI, region **`europe-west4`** (Netherlands). When you interact with Njila's conversational features, your inputs are processed by Google's Gemini large language model via a regional endpoint that contractually guarantees ML processing remains within the European Union.

### 5.2 Why your data is transferred outside Zambia

The DPA prefers personal data to be stored on servers located in Zambia. At the time of writing, no major cloud infrastructure provider operates data centers in Zambia that can support the security, scale, and AI processing requirements of Njila. We have evaluated this position carefully and have selected the European Union as the storage location for the reasons below.

### 5.3 Why the European Union

We have chosen the European Union for the following reasons:

- The EU's data protection framework (the GDPR) is widely recognized as among the most stringent in the world. It provides strong, enforceable protections for personal data and meaningful rights for individuals whose data is processed.
- The cloud providers we use — Google Cloud Platform and MongoDB Atlas — operate in the EU under contractual commitments that align with the GDPR, including binding data processing agreements and the EU Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable.
- Routing all storage and processing through a single jurisdiction with strong protections is simpler to explain, audit, and govern than splitting data across multiple jurisdictions.
- We are not currently storing data on servers in the United States. Where any of our service providers operate from the United States, they access data hosted in the EU under contractual safeguards described in Section 6.

### 5.4 Your consent to cross-border transfer

In accordance with Section 71 of the DPA, we ask for your **explicit consent** to the cross-border transfer of your personal data to the European Union as part of your registration for Njila. You may withdraw this consent at any time by contacting privacy@njila.ai, although doing so will require us to suspend or terminate your access to the Service, because storage outside Zambia is integral to how Njila is provided.

### 5.5 Safeguards

The personal data we transfer to the European Union is protected by:

- The GDPR, which directly governs the cloud infrastructure providers operating within the EU.
- Data processing agreements between Tabiya US and our infrastructure providers that incorporate the EU Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent terms.
- Encryption of data in transit and at rest.
- Access controls limiting who can access your data and for what purposes.

### 5.6 Future migration

We will continue to monitor cloud infrastructure availability in Zambia and the African region. If a viable in-country or regional option emerges that meets our security, scale, and AI processing requirements, we are committed to evaluating a migration. Any such change would be communicated to you in advance, and your rights under the DPA would not be reduced.

## 6. Sharing of Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data. We share your personal data only in the limited circumstances described below.

**Service providers.** We use third-party service providers to operate Njila, including cloud infrastructure providers (Google Cloud Platform, MongoDB Atlas), authentication services, analytics tools, and security and monitoring services. These providers act as **data processors** under our direction and are bound by written agreements that require them to protect your data and use it only to provide services to us.

**Program Partners.** Njila is delivered in partnership with the World Bank, Zambia's Ministry of Technology and Science, the Technical Education, Vocational and Entrepreneurship Training Authority (TEVET), and participating institutions. We may share aggregated or anonymized statistics with these partners to support the operation, evaluation, and reporting of the program. We do not share individually identifiable personal data with partners unless you have explicitly consented or unless required by law.

**Researchers.** Tabiya is a research-engaged nonprofit. We may share anonymized or aggregated data with academic and research partners (including the University of Oxford) to support studies that advance our charitable mission. Such sharing is governed by data sharing agreements and is subject to ethical review where applicable.

**Government and legal requests.** We may disclose personal data where required to comply with a lawful order, regulatory request, or applicable law, including under the DPA.

**Business transitions.** If Tabiya US is involved in a merger, acquisition, or transfer of assets, your personal data may be transferred to the successor entity, subject to the same protections set out in this Policy and to any notification and consent requirements under the DPA.

## 7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Policy, in accordance with the DPA.

- **Account data:** retained for the duration of your active use of Njila.
- **Skills profile and conversation history:** retained for as long as your account is active, to provide continuity across sessions.
- **Inactive accounts:** if you have not used your account for 24 months, we will notify you and, in the absence of activity or response, will delete or anonymize your personal data within a further 6 months.
- **Account deletion:** if you ask us to delete your account, we will delete your personal data within 30 days, except where we are required to retain certain data to comply with legal obligations.
- **Aggregated or anonymized data:** may be retained indefinitely for research, evaluation, and reporting purposes.

Specific retention periods may be longer where required by law or where data is needed to defend a legal claim.

## 8. Data Security

We apply reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include:

- Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest.
- Role-based access controls and authentication for staff and service providers.
- Logging and monitoring of access to systems handling personal data.
- Secure software development practices and regular review of our infrastructure.
- Contractual obligations on service providers to maintain comparable security standards.
- Internal training on data protection for staff with access to personal data.

No system is fully secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a personal data breach that is likely to affect your rights, we will notify the Zambia Data Protection Commission within **24 hours** as required by the DPA, and we will notify affected users without undue delay.

## 9. Your Rights

Under the DPA, and consistent with our voluntary commitment to GDPR-equivalent standards, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

- **Right of access.** You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
- **Right to rectification.** You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
- **Right to erasure.** You can ask us to delete your personal data. Some data may need to be retained to comply with legal obligations or to defend legal claims.
- **Right to restrict processing.** You can ask us to limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
- **Right to data portability.** You can ask us to provide your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, or to transfer it to another service where this is technically feasible.
- **Right to object.** You can object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
- **Right to withdraw consent.** Where processing is based on your consent (including the cross-border transfer described in Section 5), you can withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.
- **Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions** that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you. Njila's recommendations are advisory; decisions about your education and employment remain yours.
- **Right to lodge a complaint** with the Zambia Data Protection Commission if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@njila.ai. We will respond within 30 days, or sooner where required by law. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

## 10. Children and Young People

Njila is designed for users aged 18 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 18 without appropriate consent.

Where Njila is deployed in a setting that may include users below the age of 18 (for example, in a tertiary education institution where some students are minors), we work with the institution to ensure that appropriate parental or guardian consent is obtained and that age-appropriate protections are in place.

If you become aware that a child under 18 has provided personal data to Njila without appropriate consent, please contact privacy@njila.ai and we will take steps to delete that data.
